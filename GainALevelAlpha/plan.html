Beginner

Recently decide to get strong? Want to get big, but don't know where to start? Everyone who lifts has been right where you are now; you're new at the gym, and you don't know where to go, what to lift or how to use the machines. Well, using the experience of every lifter before you we are here to help.

Who is a beginner?

A beginner is a person that has either little to no lifting experience. During the sign up process you should have been told where we think you fall on the scale. If you are here, you are most likely a beginner. As such you should be aware of what realistic growth is, also that more or less every athlete/movie star in the world is on steroids of some sort, and to look like them takes a lot of hard work and dedication. Finally, the stronger you are the easier it is to get big, as a beginner you should focus on building your strength first then size. This is what Arnold Swatsenager says gave him his edge to win 6 Mr.Olypimias.

A Simple beginner's Routine

You will do 3 work outs per week on non consecutive days. The first work out is your heavy work out. The second work out is your medium work out, use 10% less weight for your work sets. The final work out for the week is your lite work out, use 20% less weight.

Do a lite warm up with 1/4 of your work sets weight. Do a medium warm up with 1/2 of your work sets weight. Do 2 work sets with the same weight. Choose a starting weight and start light.

These are the seven exercises you will be starting with.

Squats
Bench Presses
Bent-Over Rows
Overhead Barbell Presses
Stiff-Legged Deadlifts
Barbell Curls
Calf Raises

You will be running this program on a five week cycle as follows:
The first week do all 4 sets for 8 reps.
The second week do all 4 sets for 9 reps.
The third week do all 4 sets for 10 reps.
The fourth week do all 4 sets for 11 reps.
The fifth week do all 4 sets for 12 reps.
If you got all of the required reps on the fifth week then increase the weight by 10% and

repeat the cycle. If you didn't get all of the reps on the fifth week then repeat the cycle with the same weight. You shouldn't need more than one minute rest between the warm up sets and you shouldn't need more than one minute thirty seconds between the work sets.
Do some cardio and abs work on non weight training days.
